Fourier-Stieltjes algebra of a groupoid


Definition 0.1.

The Fourier-Stieltjes algebra of a groupoid, Gl. In ref. [3]), A.L.T. Paterson defined the Fourier-Stieltjes algebra of a groupoid, Gl, as the space of coefficients ϕ=(ξ,η), where ξ,η are
L∞-sections for some measurable Gl -Hilbert bundle (μ,ℜ,L). Thus, for x∈Gl,

ϕ⁢(x)=L⁢(x)⁢ξ⁢(s⁢(x),η⁢(r⁢(x))). (0.1)

Therefore, ϕ belongs to L∞⁢Gl=L∞⁢(Gl,ν).

References

  • 2 A. L. T. Paterson, The Fourier algebra for locally compact groupoids., Preprint, (2001).
  • 3 A. L. T. Paterson, The Fourier-Stieltjes and Fourier algebras for locally compact groupoids, (2003).
