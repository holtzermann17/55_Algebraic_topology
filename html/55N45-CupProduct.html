cup product


Let X be a topological space and R be a commutative ring. The diagonal map Δ:X→X×X induces a chain map between singular cochain complexes:

Δ*:C*⁢(X×X;R)→C*⁢(X;R)

.

Let h:C*⁢(X;R)⊗C*⁢(X;R)→C*⁢(X×X;R)

denote the chain homotopy equivalence associated with the Kunneth .

Given α∈Cp⁢(X;R) and β∈Cq⁢(X;R) we define

α⌣β=Δ*⁢h⁢(α⊗β).

As Δ* and h are chain maps, ⌣ induces a well defined product on cohomology groups, known as the cup product. Hence the direct sum of the cohomology groups of X has the structure of a ring. This is called the cohomology ring of X.
