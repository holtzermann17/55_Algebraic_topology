Hopf bundle


Consider S3⊂ℝ4=ℂ2. The structure of ℂ2 gives a map ℂ2-{0}→ℂ⁢P1, the complex projective line by the natural projection. Since ℂ⁢P1 is homeomorphic to S2, by restriction to S3, we get a map π:S3→S2. We call this the Hopf bundle.

This is a principal S1-bundle (http://planetmath.org/PrincipalBundle), and a generator of π3⁢(S2). From the long exact sequence of the bundle (http://planetmath.org/LongExactSequenceLocallyTrivialBundle):

⋯⁢πn⁢(S1)→πn⁢(S3)→πn⁢(S2)→⋯

we get that πn⁢(S3)≅πn⁢(S2) for all n≥3. In particular, π3⁢(S2)≅π3⁢(S3)≅ℤ.
