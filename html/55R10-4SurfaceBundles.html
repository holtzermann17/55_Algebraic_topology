4 surface bundles


Four Kleinbottle bundles K⊂M→S1.

There are four because the extended mapping class group for the genus two, non orientable surface K the Klein bottle, is ℤ2⊕ℤ2.

This group is generated by a Dehn-twist τ about the unique two-sided curve in K and by the y-homeomorphism, both representing two isotopy classes of order two.

These bundles are

  • •

    K×S1, the trivial Cartesian product

  • •

    K×τS1,

  • •

    K×yS1=K×∼IO∪(0,1)M⁢o¨×S1,

  • •

    K×y⁢τS1.

Where K×∼IO is the orientable twisted I-bundle over K, among the three I-bundles over K.The symbol ∪(0,1) is used to indicate that, the meridian in ∂⁡(M⁢o¨×S1) is attached to the meridian of ∂⁡(K×∼IO), both 2-tori. M⁢o¨ is the Möbius band.

Now, since those monodromies are periodic then they are also homeomorphic respectively to the Seifert fiber spaces

  • •

    (NnI,2|0)=K×S1,

  • •

    (NnI,2|1)=(K×S1∖intW)∪(1,1)W,

  • •

    (NnII,2|0)=K×yS1=K×∼IO∪(0,1)Mo¨×S1 and

  • •

    (NnII,2|1)=(K×yS1∖intW)∪(1,1)W

Where W is a solid torus in the space and ∪(1,1) is the Dehn surgery: meridian of ∂⁡W to the longitude of ∂⁡(K×S1∖int⁢W).

The non trivial homeomorphisms were given by Per Orlik and Frank Raymond, in 1969.
