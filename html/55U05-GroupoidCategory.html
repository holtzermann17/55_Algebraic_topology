groupoid category


Definition 0.1.

Groupoid categories, or categories of groupoids, can be defined simply by considering a groupoid as a category 𝒢1 with all invertible morphisms, and objects defined by the groupoid class or set of groupoid elements; then, the groupoid category, 𝒢2, is defined as the 2-category whose objects are 𝒢1 categories (groupoids), and whose morphisms are functors of 𝒢1 categories consistent with the definition of groupoid homomorphisms, or in the case of topological groupoids, consistent as well with topological groupoid homeomorphisms (http://planetmath.org/Homeomorphism).

Example 0.1 : The 2-category of Lie groupoids is an example of a groupoid category, or 2-category of groupoids.

Definition 0.2.

The 2-category of Lie groupoids GL has Lie groupoids as objects, and for any two such objects 𝐆𝐋 and 𝐇𝐋 there is a hom-category

h⁢o⁢m⁢(𝐆𝐋,𝐇𝐋)=B⁢B⁢(𝐆𝐋,𝐇𝐋),

where B⁢B⁢(𝐆𝐋,𝐇𝐋), is a category whose objects are 𝐆𝐋–𝐇𝐋 bibundles of the Lie groupoids 𝐆𝐋 and 𝐇𝐋, respectively over M and N, and whose morphisms are arrows f:E→E′ between such bibundles E and E′ that commute with the bundles π1:E→M and π2:E′→N:

\xymatrix⁢E⁢\ar⁢[r⁢r]f⁢\ar⁢[d⁢r]π1⁢&⁢&⁢E′⁢\ar⁢[d⁢l]π2⁢&⁢M⁢&
\xymatrix⁢E⁢\ar⁢[r⁢r]f⁢\ar⁢[d⁢r]π1′⁢&⁢&⁢E′⁢\ar⁢[d⁢l]π2′⁢&⁢N⁢&,

consistent respectively with the 𝐆𝐋– and 𝐇𝐋– actions. Moreover, the composition of two bibundles is given by the Hilsum-Skandalis product.

Remark 0.1 : The 2-category of groupoids 𝒢2, plays a central role in the generalised, categorical Galois theory involving fundamental groupoid functors.
