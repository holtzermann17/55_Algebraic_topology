0.1 Cubically thin homotopy

Let u,u′ be squares in X with common vertices.

  1. 1.

    A cubically thin homotopy U:u≡T□u′ between u and u′ is a cube (http://planetmath.org/Polyhedron) U∈R3□⁢(X) such that

    • –

      U is a homotopy between u and u′,

      i.e. ∂1-⁡(U)=u,∂1+⁡(U)=u′,

    • –

      U is rel. vertices of I2,

      i.e. ∂2-⁡∂2-⁡(U),∂2-⁡∂2+⁡(U),∂2+⁡∂2-⁡(U),∂2+⁡∂2+⁡(U) are constant,

    • –

      the faces ∂iα⁡(U) are thin for α=±1,i=1,2.

  2. 2.

    The square u is cubically T-equivalent to u′, denoted u≡T□u′ if there is a cubically thin homotopy between u and u′.

This definition enables one to construct the homotopy double groupoid scheme 𝝆2□⁢(X) , by defining a relation of cubically thin homotopy on the set R2□⁢(X) of squares.
